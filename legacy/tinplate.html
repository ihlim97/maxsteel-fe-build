Tinplate

Background

Tinplate written as TP in short is form of coated steel. TP is used widely for its corrosion resistance and attractive shiny surface. The manufacturing process of TP involves tinning of the black plate. The black plate is usually cold rolled steel.

Tinning or tinplating is simply the coating of Tin onto the black plate a.k.a. base metal. The base metal can either be single cold reduced or double cold reduced depending on the hardness required. Normally, single cold reduced base metal is used.

There are two main tinning processes for TP, electrolytic and hot dipped tinplating. Electrolytic tinplating is the electroplating of tin onto the base metal via ion transfer and hot dipped tinplating is the coating of tin onto the base metal. Most TP now goes through electrolytic tinplating.

Post treatment processes are carried out to achieve different temper grades, surface finishes and some other properties.

Other similar alternatives for tinplate include tin-free steel or terne-steel which combines both tin and lead on its coating.

Characteristics

The general appearance for most TP is shiny and polished silver finish. TP can be produced with a few finishes that include bright, stone, silver and matte. Common attributes include superb corrosion resistance, formability, solderability, weldability and printability. Corrosion resistance is sustained as long as the Tin surface remains unbroken.

Applications

TP is mainly used in food & beverages cans, general cans, caps, cables, electronic parts and home appliances. TP is generally chosen for its appearance and corrosion resistivity.
