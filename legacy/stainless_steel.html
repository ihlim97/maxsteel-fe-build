Stainless Steel

Background

Stainless steel also written as SUS or STS in short is highly resistant to corrosion in a variety of environments. SUS is a high alloy steel with a predominant concentration of at least 11% Chromium by weight. Chromium forms an invisible passivation Chromium Oxide (Cr2O3) layer which protects the surface from oxidation effects. Properties are further enhanced with addition of Nickel and Molybdenum.

SUS is available in numerous grades to suit almost any form of steel application; SUS can undergo surface treatments to obtain different surface textures for various applications. Surface treatments include polishing or grinding to desired textural finish. Production of SUS has similar processes to cold/hot rolled steel.

SUS are divided into three main classes on the basis of their microstructure. They are namely, austenitic, ferritic and martensitic stainless steels and can be produced from either cold or hot rolled based steel. With so many different grades, SUS offers a wide range of mechanical properties combined with excellent corrosion resistance, thus making the steel very versatile in its applicability.

Characteristics

The general appearance of SUS is a clean and neat surface. Surface finish has a wide variety including mirror, dull, hair line, etched, embossed, chemically colored and many others. Its surface has excellent corrosion resistance and is resilient towards strong pollution or corrosive environments. Common attributes other than corrosive resistivity, include excellent wear resistivity, strength and toughness.

Applications

SUS is used in many applications, which includes electrical appliances, computer parts, automotive, construction, industrial, kitchenware, furniture, food facilities, intricate internal parts, sculptures and many others.
