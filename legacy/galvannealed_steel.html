Galvannealed Steel

Background

Galvannealed steel also written as GA in short is similar to hot dipped galvanized steel but it has an additional annealing process. After the hot dip galvanizing process, the galvanized steel is reheated in a galvannealing furnace. The reheating initiates thermal diffusion that causes the pure Zinc coated layer to form an Iron-Zinc alloy with the Iron underneath.

GA can have different pre and post treatments during their manufacturing processes. This allows the choice on appearances, resistances, lubrications and characteristics. GA offered by MaxSteel is certified 100% chromate-free and ROHS compliant.

Characteristics

The general appearance of GA is a fine grayish matte finish. The fine matte finish acts well as a primer for painting. GA does not peel off its galvanized coating during forming, stamping or bending. Common attributes include paintability, weldability, formability and excellent corrosion resistance.

Applications

GA is mainly used in electrical appliances, office equipment, furniture, computers, automotive, signage and some outdoor applications. GA is economical to industries that do paint works on steel as no chemical treatment is required before painting.
