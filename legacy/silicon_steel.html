Silicon Steel

Background

Silicon steel is also known as electrical steel. Other names include laminated steel, transformer steel or silicon electrical steel. This special steel is suited to produce specific magnetic properties like low core losses and high permeabilities.

Silicon steel is an Iron alloy with a small percentage of Silicon of not more than 6.5% usually. The presence of Silicon increases the electrical resistivity of steel and reduces core losses. Silicon steel is produced in the same manner as most steels except for the direction controlled rolling for grain oriented Silicon steel.

There are two main types of Silicon steel, grain oriented (GO) and the non grain oriented (NO).

Characteristics

For GO, the direction controlled rolling relative to the crystal microstructure results in magnetic density increments.

As for NO, there is no grain orientation hence it is anisotropic and magnetic directions properties are in all directions.

Silicon steel is usually coated to further increase the resistivity. The coating also provides resistance to corrosion and lubrication during processing. Coating can either be organic or inorganic depending on the application.

Common attributes include good corrosion resistance and punchability.

Applications

GO is mainly used for power transformer and distribution. Products like the cores of high-efficiency transformers, electric motors and generators.

NO is mainly used for electrical appliances or when high-efficiency is less important and components which utilize more of anisotropic properties of Silicon steel.
