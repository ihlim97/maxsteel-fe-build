Galvalume Steel

Background

Galvalume steel is commonly known in the market as Aluminum-Zinc or AZ in short. The base metal is normally cold rolled steel, hot rolled based also exists. Its technology is licensed by BIEC International, Inc.

The coating on AZ consists of 55% Aluminum, 40+% Zinc and perhaps a small percentage of Silicon. AZ is a high alloy coated steel and has several unique characteristics which make it very suitable in a wide range of applications. Various post surface treatments are added to customize different requirements.

Characteristics

The general appearance of AZ is bright gray in color with distinctively silvery-white fine spangles. AZ has superior anti-corrosion properties due to the compact Aluminum Oxide film and the galvanic Zinc content. Unique to AZ, heat reflectivity and resistance to heat discoloration makes it a choice material for special applications. Common attributes include superb corrosion resistance, heat reflectivity, paintability, formability and beautiful aesthetics.

Applications

AZ is mainly used in electrical appliances, automotive, roofing, construction, furniture, architectural, gutters and many others. AZ is popular to many industries due to its versatility, ease of use, aesthetics and long-term performance.
