Hot Rolled Steel

Background

Hot rolled steel also written as HR in short is the precursor for cold rolled steel products and other forms of steel. HR raw material begins with the steel slab produced from continuous casting. The slab first enters the reheating furnace to reach the rolling temperature, surface scales are normally removed at this point. Once heated up, the slab is rolled to width in the roughing mill.

After that, it goes to the finishing mill for thickness and run-out table for cooling. Lastly, it will go through skin pass for inspection and some shape correction before coiling into the HR product.

HR is normally pickled and oiled to prevent oxidation

Characteristics

The general appearance of HR is usually dark gray and dull. HR has four main grades with the SPHC grade as commercial quality and the rest as drawing quality. The different grades are produced through manipulation of chemical compositions. Common attributes include drawability, bendability and structural strength.

Applications

HR is mainly used as the base metal for cold rolled steel, general structures, shipping containers, ships, tubes, pipes, automotive, high pressure cylinders, oil wells, special applications and others.
