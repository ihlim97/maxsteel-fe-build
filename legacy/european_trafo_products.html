European Trafo Products

Company Profile

European Trafo Products Private Limited is incorporated on 23rd July 1998 as a Joint-Venture of MaxSteel Enterprise Private Limited (Singapore) and Cedaspe s.p.a. (Italy).

European Trafo Products or ETP in short, serves as a regional distributor and stockist of quality transformer accessories for indoor and outdoor transformers for the South-east Asian markets. ETP supplies products based on DIN, EN and British and Industry standards. The company has storage facilities and ensures on time delivery based on customers’ production plans and forecasts.

Since inauguration in 1998, ETP has been providing products of top European quality standards to various established transformer manufacturers in Southeast Asia as well as in Hong Kong and Japan.

ETP has hosted several seminars in various countries around the region so as to allow active participations with exchanges of commercial and technical knowledge between participants and suppliers. ETP has also been participating in exhibitions in Asia to introduce products to potential customers.

The company is looking forward to hold seminars every alternate year to allow further interaction and discussions so as to improve the quality of our products.


 ETP is formed by companies of unique strengths
MaxSteel



MaxSteel’s main business activity is on metal processing. Having the equipment and facilities of a full-fledged metal processing center, the company is able to process transformer core steels into desired coils or sheets.

Based in Singapore, the company is also a good foundation for distributing ETP’s products around the Asian region.

Cedaspe



 Cedaspe s.p.a manufactures bushings and accessories based on the European Union (EN) standards. Cedaspe has been manufacturing bushings and transformer accessories for over 40 years; Cedaspe is one of the world’s most renowned companies in this particular sector of the market.

Their experience in this field is consolidated by over 100 years of experience that Cedaspe has gained directly from its origins as CED Elettromeccanica.
