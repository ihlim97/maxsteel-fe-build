Cold Rolled Steel

Background

Cold rolled steel also written as CR in short is the base metal for many commercial steels in the market. Cold rolling is a process in which hot rolled coil undergoes deformation by passing through rollers at below its re-crystallization temperature. This will increase the material strength due to crystal structure defects and thickness rolled down precisely to the desired value.

Hot rolled coils are usually pickled in acid before cold rolling and after cold rolling, processes like cleaning, annealing and tempering takes place to obtain the required specifications.

Rust preventive oil has to be applied to the finished CR product before packaging as it does not have cathodic protection.

Characteristics

The general appearance of CR is usually gray and comes in two surface finish options. The dull finish which is matt and the bright finish which is smooth. By manipulating the chemical composition when producing the base for CR and varying the tempering, a range of CR grades can be produced with different mechanical properties. Common attributes include drawability, formability and paintability.

Applications

CR is mainly used as base metal for many steel products, automotives, electrical appliances, office equipment, computer parts, furniture, structural applications, high tensile steel and many others.
