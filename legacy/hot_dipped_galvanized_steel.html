Hot Dipped Galvanized Steel

Background

Hot dipped galvanized steel is commonly known in the market as galvanized iron or GI in short. The base material for GI is normally cold rolled steel, hot rolled based GI is also available.

As its name suggests, the base metal undergoes galvanizing by dipping and immersing the steel into molten zinc followed by high air pressure spraying from air knives to achieve appropriate coating. Galvanized steel goes through skin pass process to obtain flat and bright surfaces.

GI can have different base metals and different pre and post treatments during their manufacturing processes. This allows the choice on appearances, resistances and characteristics. GI offered by MaxSteel is certified 100% chromate-free and ROHS compliant.

Characteristics

The general appearance of GI can be quite varying, as it can have coating surface finishes of different degrees of spangle. They are namely zero spangle, minimized spangle or regular spangle which resulted from zinc crystal growth conditions. GI has a smooth finish and is relatively bright and shiny compared to EG. Common attributes include excellent corrosion resistance due to high sacrificial zinc coatings, weldability and formability.

Applications

GI is mainly used for electrical appliances, office equipments, computers, automotive parts, furniture, construction, outdoors, and base for pre-painted steel and others.
